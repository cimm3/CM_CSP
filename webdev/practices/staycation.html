Bryce Canyon National Park

Why go?

Bryce National Park is located in southwestern Utah, and is one of the coolest rock formations. It is well known for its unparalled landscapes, cretaing the largest concentrartions of hoodoos. The park offers greta stagazing sites, camps and loads of trails. It is most well known for the vibrant red color of the rocks, and the rocks form a "natural city" due to their structure.

The Most famous trail

It’s easy to see why this trail is one of the most popular in the park. It starts with a horizon-sweeping view at Sunset Point, then makes its way past the Silent City — a rock metropolis of hoodoos and spires — to Wall Street, where the trail heads into a narrow slot canyon surrounded by sweeping sandstone walls. The walk is around 1.5 miles and the best times to visit are around April - October.
